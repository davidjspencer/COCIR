Programme

The Summit will be moderated by John Bowis, Former MEP and UK Health Minister

09:30 - 10:00
Registration & coffee
10:00 (?) - 09:10
Welcome and Introduction
09:10 – 09:30
Welcome address
09:30 - 10:30
Panel debate 1: Scaling up digital health More Info
×

Abstract: The transformation towards integrated care and data sharing requires the deployment of digital health technologies at scale. Digital health technologies are an enabler of this journey through its different phases: from data capture, data aggregation and sharing to care coordination and population health, digital technologies are critical to deliver this transformation. The session will focus on two key themes: securing political leadership and financing to reach the scale needed to translate this health data richness into transformative action.

10:30 – 11:15
Coffee
11:15 – 12:15
Panel debate 2: Sharing data, collaborating and coordinating across the continuum of care More Info
×

Abstract: A vast amount of health data is currently being captured by EHRs systems. Transforming this information into knowledge to improve quality of healthcare delivery and health systems performance, is to a large extent a matter of will. This session will explore the views of different stakeholders, their needs and expectations with a view to understand what steps need to be taken to achieve the scale required to deliver transformational change.

12:30 - 13:30
Networking Lunch
13.30-14.30
Panel debate 3: Unleashing Intelligence for Integrated Care and Population health More Info
×

Abstract: Enhanced connectivity makes it possible to combine diverse and widely distributed data sources into a combined “data lake”. The application of Data Analytics and Artificial Intelligence will unleash the knowledge and create insights to support value-based healthcare. This panel will discuss the main challenges, levers and requirements to unlock the potential of Data Analytics and Artificial Intelligence to support care delivery at the point of care, precision medicine, population health management and research.

14:30 - 15:30
Panel debate 4: Managing Data Protection and Cybersecurity in healthcare More Info
×

Abstract: By May 25 2018 all organisations handling EU citizens’ personal data will need to adapt their processes, products and services to become compliant with General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). At the same time, by May 9 2018, all Member States should have transposed the Network Information Security Directive. The session will focus on how to effectively manage GDPR and cybersecurity requirements in the health care sector to enable trust, transparency and safety.

15:30
Closing address
15:55 - 16:00
Concluding remarks
Nicole Denjoy, COCIR Secretary General
